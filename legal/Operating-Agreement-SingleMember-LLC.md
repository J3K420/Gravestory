### ARTICLE 1 — FORMATION

**1.1 Formation.** This Limited Liability Company, **[LLC LEGAL NAME], LLC** (the "Company"), was formed as a single-member limited liability company under the South Carolina Uniform Limited Liability Company Act (S.C. Code Ann. § 33-44-101 *et seq.*, the "Act") by the filing of Articles of Organization with the South Carolina Secretary of State on **[DATE ARTICLES FILED]**.

**1.2 Name.** The name of the Company is **[LLC LEGAL NAME], LLC**.

**1.3 Principal Office.** The principal office and designated office of the Company is **[STREET ADDRESS, CITY, SC, ZIP]**, or such other place as the Member may designate from time to time.

**1.4 Registered Agent and Office.** The Company's initial registered agent in South Carolina is **[REGISTERED AGENT NAME]**, with a registered office at **[REGISTERED AGENT STREET ADDRESS, CITY, SC, ZIP]**. The Member may change the registered agent or office at any time as permitted by the Act.

**1.5 Purpose.** The Company is formed to engage in any lawful business, act, or activity for which a limited liability company may be organized under the Act, including, without limitation, the development, publication, distribution, and monetization of mobile and web software applications and related services.

**1.6 Term.** The Company commenced on the date its Articles of Organization were filed and shall continue perpetually until dissolved in accordance with this Agreement or the Act.

---

### ARTICLE 2 — THE MEMBER

**2.1 Sole Member.** The sole member of the Company is **[YOUR FULL LEGAL NAME]** (the "Member"). The Member owns one hundred percent (100%) of the membership interest in the Company.

**2.2 Single-Member Intent.** The Company is intended to be a single-member limited liability company. For United States federal income tax purposes, the Company shall be treated as a **disregarded entity** (i.e., its income and expenses are reported on the Member's individual return), unless and until the Member elects a different tax classification by filing the appropriate election with the Internal Revenue Service.

**2.3 Limited Liability.** The Member shall not be personally liable for any debt, obligation, or liability of the Company solely by reason of being the Member, except to the extent required by the Act. The Member intends that the Company be treated as a legal entity separate and distinct from the Member.

**2.4 Additional Members.** Additional members may be admitted only with the written consent of the Member. Upon admission of any additional member, the Member shall amend this Agreement to reflect the rights and obligations of multiple members.

---

### ARTICLE 3 — MANAGEMENT

**3.1 Member-Managed.** The Company is **member-managed**. The Member has full, exclusive, and complete authority and discretion to manage and control the business and affairs of the Company, to make all decisions regarding the Company, and to perform any and all acts customary or incidental to the management of the Company's business.

**3.2 Authority to Bind.** The Member is authorized, acting alone, to execute and deliver on behalf of the Company any contract, instrument, or document, including (without limitation) opening and operating bank accounts, entering into agreements with vendors and platforms (including app-store, payment-processing, and cloud-service providers), and taking any action the Member deems necessary or appropriate.

**3.3 Officers (Optional).** The Member may, but is not required to, designate one or more officers (such as a President, Treasurer, or Secretary) and delegate authority to them. Absent such designation, the Member holds all such authority.

---

### ARTICLE 4 — CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS

**4.1 Initial Contribution.** The Member has contributed, or shall contribute, initial capital to the Company in the amount or property described on the Company's records (an initial contribution may be a nominal cash amount, e.g., the opening bank deposit).

**4.2 No Obligation for Additional Contributions.** The Member is not obligated to make any additional capital contribution. The Member may make additional contributions at any time, in any amount, in the Member's sole discretion, and such contributions shall be recorded in the Company's books.

**4.3 No Interest.** No interest shall accrue on, or be payable with respect to, any capital contribution.

---

### ARTICLE 5 — DISTRIBUTIONS, PROFITS, AND LOSSES

**5.1 Allocation.** Because the Company has a single Member, all profits and losses of the Company are allocated entirely to the Member.

**5.2 Distributions (Owner's Draws).** The Member may take distributions ("owner's draws") of cash or property from the Company at any time and in any amount, in the Member's sole discretion, provided the Company remains able to pay its debts as they become due in the ordinary course of business after the distribution (consistent with § 33-44-406 of the Act). Distributions are made by transferring funds from a Company account to the Member; the Company shall not pay the Member's personal expenses directly.

**5.3 Separateness of Funds.** The Company shall maintain its own bank account(s) in the Company's name and shall keep its funds separate from the personal funds of the Member. The Member shall not commingle Company funds with personal funds. All Company income shall be deposited into, and all Company expenses paid from, Company accounts.

---

### ARTICLE 6 — BOOKS, RECORDS, AND TAXES

**6.1 Books and Records.** The Company shall keep accurate books and records of its business and financial condition at its principal office.

**6.2 Fiscal Year.** The Company's fiscal year is the calendar year, unless otherwise required by law or elected by the Member.

**6.3 Taxes.** The Member is responsible for reporting the Company's income and paying all applicable taxes, including federal and South Carolina income tax and self-employment tax, on the Company's net income, whether or not distributions are made.

---

### ARTICLE 7 — LIABILITY, INDEMNIFICATION, AND SEPARATENESS

**7.1 Limited Liability of the Member.** Except as otherwise required by the Act, the debts, obligations, and liabilities of the Company are solely the debts, obligations, and liabilities of the Company, and the Member is not personally liable for them.

**7.2 Indemnification.** To the fullest extent permitted by the Act, the Company shall indemnify and hold harmless the Member (and any officer the Member designates) from and against any claim, liability, loss, or expense arising out of any act or omission performed in good faith on behalf of the Company, except for acts of fraud, willful misconduct, or knowing violation of law.

**7.3 Maintenance of Separate Entity.** The Member shall observe the formalities of operating the Company as a separate legal entity, including maintaining separate bank accounts, keeping Company records, signing Company contracts in the Company's name, and not commingling assets. The Member acknowledges that observing these formalities supports the limited-liability protection afforded by the Act.

---

### ARTICLE 8 — DISSOLUTION

**8.1 Events of Dissolution.** The Company shall dissolve upon the written election of the Member, or upon any event requiring dissolution under the Act.

**8.2 Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Member shall wind up the Company's affairs, liquidate its assets, pay or provide for its debts and liabilities, and distribute any remaining assets to the Member. The Member shall then file Articles of Termination with the South Carolina Secretary of State.

---

### ARTICLE 9 — GENERAL PROVISIONS

**9.1 Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by, and shall be construed in accordance with, the laws of the State of South Carolina.

**9.2 Amendment.** This Agreement may be amended at any time by a written instrument signed by the Member.

**9.3 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement of the Member with respect to the Company and supersedes any prior understanding or agreement.

**9.4 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

**9.5 Effect of the Act.** To the extent this Agreement is silent on any matter, the provisions of the Act shall govern.
