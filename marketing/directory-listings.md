## 3. SKIP list — do not submit to these

- **Product Hunt launch day** — a low-intent install spike from tech tourists can *hurt* 2026 Play ranking via uninstall weighting (`gtm-strategy.md` §3 SKIP list); 6-8 hours for an audience that won't scan a grave.
- **BetaList** — pre-launch site; GraveStory launched June 29, 2026, so it's ineligible and the audience is wrong anyway. *(This supersedes the passing "AlternativeTo/BetaList" mention in `gtm-strategy.md` §5 — AlternativeTo yes, BetaList no.)*
- **APK mirror sites** (APKPure, APKMirror, Aptoide, etc.) — never submit; unofficial builds destroy trust and can carry tampered binaries. If one scrapes the listing on its own, that's theirs; we never feed them.
- **Paid directory placements** — near-zero real pickup (same verdict as paid PR wires in `gtm-strategy.md` §5); a free editorial email beats every one of them.

## NEVER-SAY compliance (what every entry above deliberately does not say)

- Never "the only AI gravestone scanner" or any scan-superiority claim — BillionGraves, MyHeritage Scribe, and Stories in Stone all scan; the anchors describe the shared user moment instead.
- Never "reads the stone" as the differentiator (Find A Grave added OCR Jan 2026) — reading is the mechanism; the source-cited STORY is the claim.
- Never a flat "nobody writes a life story" and never "competitors can't write a biography" — the claims here stay scoped to what GraveStory does, not what rivals lack.
- Never competitor prices, never an iPhone app, never guaranteed bio depth ("when records come up thin…" is stated in the description itself), never "AI" as the lead, never portraits/faces of the ordinary dead (portraits aren't mentioned at all).
- No usage numbers anywhere — none exist.
