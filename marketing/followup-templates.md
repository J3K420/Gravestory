## Send discipline (read this every time, 30 seconds)

- **ONE follow-up per target, ever.** Silence after the follow-up means the pitch is dead — reallocate, don't re-pitch (gtm §3 kill criteria).
- **Never nudge Family Tree Magazine inside their 6–8-week window** (template 3 has the calendar note).
- **A "yes" cools fast.** Template 4 goes out within the hour, then the `reviewer-onepager.md` flow.
- Every word below is scrubbed against the NEVER-SAY list in `gtm-strategy.md` §2. Don't ad-lib new claims into a template.

**WHEN TO SEND:** any time an editor, reviewer, forum commenter, or reader raises the AI-fabrication worry — this is the reusable kernel answer; adapt the greeting, keep the substance.

**Subject:** Re: (their thread) — fair question, here's exactly how it works

> Hi [NAME],
>
> Fair question — it's the right one to ask.
>
> GraveStory doesn't ask an AI to imagine a life. It reads the stone, then searches named public records — WikiTree, Wikidata, Chronicling America newspapers, Internet Archive county histories, Wikipedia — and the story is written from what those searches return, with the sources cited so you can check them yourself.

>
> Happy to show you a scan of any stone you pick.
>
