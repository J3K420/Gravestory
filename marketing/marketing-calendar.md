## Ground rules (30 seconds, every time)

- **Every entry is optional and ≤1 hour.** If a month's action doesn't fit the hour, it doesn't happen. No entry ever creates a content treadmill.
- **Reuse only.** Each action re-sends or re-angles an asset that already exists. Writing a new campaign is never the action.
- **Never self-post** into r/Genealogy, Find-A-Grave groups, or any genealogy community (`gtm-strategy.md` §3 SKIP list — being *featured* is fine; *you* posting is the ban move). Every action below is a pitch to an editor, reporter, or creator.
- **One follow-up per target, ever** (`followup-templates.md` send discipline). A seasonal hook is a reason to pitch a *new* target or spend an *unused* follow-up — never a third email.
- Any re-angled sentence gets scrubbed against the NEVER-SAY list in `gtm-strategy.md` §2 before sending.

## The recurring Top 3 — the moments genuinely worth the hour

> **1. Memorial Day — Monday, May 31, 2027.** The single biggest cemetery-visiting moment in the
> US: flag placements on veterans' graves, national-cemetery ceremonies, families at the family
> plot, and the year's biggest cemetery-press window (stories run the week before; pitch 3–5 weeks
> ahead). **The hour happens in late April:** re-send `local-press-pitch.md` to a named local
> reporter with a flags-and-family-plots angle, and send one Memorial Day feature idea + the demo
> clip to the warmest genealogy-media contact from the pitch batches. If you do exactly one thing
> off this calendar all year, do this.
>
> **2. Family History Month — all of October 2026.** THE month genealogy outlets, libraries, and
> societies want content (a 2001 Senate-resolution observance, actively promoted by FamilySearch and
> the National Genealogical Society). Pitch 2–4 weeks ahead: **early-to-mid September is the hour.**
> The action: an unused, already-approved target may receive its scheduled pitch with a "for your Family
> History Month coverage" line, and any still-unused one-per-target follow-up from the earlier
> batches gets spent on the same hook (except Family Tree Magazine — never inside its 6–8-week
> no-nudge window; see `followup-templates.md` §3).
>
> **3. Halloween week — Saturday, October 31, 2026.** A *Saturday* Halloween means peak
> cemetery-tourism weekend (historic-cemetery tours, lantern walks), rolling straight into
> All Saints' (Sun, Nov 1) and All Souls' (Mon, Nov 2) — a three-day cemetery-visiting
> super-weekend. Local human-interest press runs "stories behind the stones" features every late
> October. If the September 17 local pitch was not sent, the hour is one named reporter with the angle
> "the real stories in our cemeteries — history, no ghosts required." If it was sent, preserve its response
> window and use only the allowed follow-up when due; do not create a second cold local-press wave.

## Skip guilt-free

This calendar is opportunistic seasoning, nothing more. The baseline plan — the thing that actually
has to happen — is `gtm-strategy.md`'s dated roadmap, and it is designed to fit one preparation day
plus short send/reply blocks. Missing a moment here costs nothing: the moments repeat every year, the assets
don't expire, and the pitches already sent keep compounding (or they don't) regardless of what any
given month's row says. Four of twelve months are deliberately "nothing — build" — that is a
feature, not a gap. If a month arrives and the hour isn't there, close this file and go build.
