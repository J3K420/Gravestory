## What every draft deliberately does NOT say (NEVER-SAY compliance)

- Never "AI writes the biography" or leads with "AI" — verified records + citations are the trust device. (One scoped exception, by design: the Family History AI Show draft talks citation mechanics to an AI-literate audience — but it still leads with the sourced story and never claims "the only AI scanner.")
- Never "reads the stone" as the *differentiator* — Find A Grave added OCR (Jan 2026); the cited STORY is the claim.
- Never states MyHeritage Scribe's limitations as fact — Scribe appears only as the news peg the shows themselves covered; the differentiation is what GraveStory does, never what Scribe lacks.
- Never asks The Genealogy Guys for sponsor-adjacent promotion — MyHeritage promotes on the show; the draft names the relationship and scopes the ask to news/interview material, no pay either direction.
- Never a flat "nobody writes a life story" — CWGC's For Evermore exists; the claim is scoped to "an *ordinary* person" from a gravestone scan.
- Never "competitors have no map" — Stories in Stone has a private one; we say "free public community map."
- Never competitor prices as fact — no competitor pricing appears in any draft; only our own is ever stated.
- Never implies an iPhone app, faces/photos of the ordinary dead, or a guaranteed rich bio on every stone.
- Never a usage statistic — no install/user/story numbers exist, so none appear anywhere.
- Never a guessed contact address — the one UNKNOWN (Family History Fanatics email) is tagged **[VERIFY CONTACT BEFORE SENDING]**, not invented.
