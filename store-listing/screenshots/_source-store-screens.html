Marie Skłodowska Curie
1867 – 1934
◍ The Panthéon, Paris, France
Life Story

Born Maria Skłodowska in Warsaw in 1867, under a Russian occupation that barred women from its universities, she taught herself in a clandestine "Flying University." [1]

In 1891 she travelled to Paris and enrolled at the Sorbonne, where she often studied so late and ate so little that she fainted from hunger over her books. [2]

Investigating the mysterious rays given off by uranium, she coined the term "radioactivity" and isolated two new elements — polonium and radium. [1][3]
